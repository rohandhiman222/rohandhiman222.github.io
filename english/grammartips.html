# Grammar Tips

# 1. A name start with a capital letter. फिर चाहे वो Sentence में कहीं पर भी आये

  • Examples
    • Ram is here.
    • Is Ram there?
    • Is that Ram?

# 2. I ( मैं ) Sentence में कहीं पर भी आये, वो कैपिटल में ही लिखा जायेगा

  • Examples
    • I don't know.
    • Why am I doing this?
    • You and I will go.

# 3. किसी भी Sentence की शुरुआत Capital Letter से और अंत full stop / period (.)

  • Examples
    • I teach English.
    • Do you learn English?
    • What a great idea!

# 4.A. The structure of a Basic Positive Sentence is ... [Subject + verb + object]

  • Examples
    • You learn English.
    • I like oranges.
    • Meera goes to school.

# 4.B. The structure of a Basic negative Sentence is.... [Subject+helping verb+not+main verb+object]

  • Examples
    • You don't learn English.
    • Meera does not go to school

# 4.C. Interrogative Sentence ...[Question word+helping verb+Subject +main verb+object]

  • Examples
    • Why do you learn English?
    • Do you learng English?

# 5. हर Sentence में 1 Subject और 1 Verb होना जरुरी है | Object optional है

  • Examples
    • Ram learns English.
    • Ram goes.

लेकिन Imparative Sentence में जब Subject नहीं होता तो वहां माना जाता है

  • Examples
    • Go! (you go!)
    • Say! (you say!)

# 6. The Subject and verb must agree in number. (Singular Subject - Singular verb) (Plural Subject - Plural Verb)

  • Examples
    • Ram works in a company.
    • Ram and rohan work in a company. the are friends.

# 7. जब दो Singular Subject Or से जुड़े हों, Use a Singular verb.

  • Examples
    • Ram or Rohan lives in Delhi.

ऐसे ही Either... or और Neither...nor के साथ भी Singular verb आती है

  • Examples
    • Either Ram or Rohan lives in Delhi
    • Neither Ram nor Ram lives in Delhi

# 8. [It's and its] both are different.

It's = it is/ it has

  • It's getting dark.
  • It's been so song nice we last met.

Its = इसका (belonging)

  • The cat hurt its paw.

# 9. Your and you're sound the same.

your = तुम्हारा

  • This is your bag.

you're = you are

  • You're welcome!
  • You're doing good.

# 10. There, Their and They're sound the same.

There = Adverb (वहां)

  • Look there!
  • There is somebody

Their = Passessive determiner (उनका/उनकी )

  • It's their house.

They're = Contraction of they are

  • They're going home.

# 11. Position of Apostrophe(') => ('s) or (s')

  • The boy's car लड़के की कार
  • The boys' car लड़को की कार

(') => to show possession (का/की/के) (who is the owner to something)

Ram's chair राम की कुर्सी

(i) For single owners, use an apostrophe+s ('s)

  • Meera's doll

(ii) For Plural owners / Put apostrophe at the end ('s)

  • The girls' doll.
  • My parrents' house.

(iii) For plural owners that don't end in 's' / Use an apostrophe +s ('s)

  • Men's books
  • People's opinions
